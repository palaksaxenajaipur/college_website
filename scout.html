About Scout & Guide

Scouts and Guides are global youth organizations aiming to develop young people’s character, leadership skills, and citizenship through outdoor activities, community service, and education. Founded by Robert Baden-Powell and his sister Agnes Baden-Powell in the early 20th century, Scouts and Guides have grown into one of the world’s largest and most influential youth movements. Members, known as Scouts or Guides, participate in various activities such as camping, hiking, first aid training, and environmental conservation projects. These organizations promote values such as teamwork, self-reliance, respect for others, and stewardship of the environment. Scouts and Guides provide young people with opportunities to learn, grow, and make positive contributions to their communities, fostering a sense of belonging and responsibility that lasts a lifetime.

Aim of Scouts & Guides

  • Develop leadership and teamwork skills.
  • Promote discipline, patriotism, and social responsibility.
  • Encourage community service and voluntary work.
  • Build physical fitness and life skills.
  • Helping young people develop physically, mentally, socially, and spiritually.
  • Character Building: Instilling values like integrity, responsibility, and respect for others.

Scouts and Guides Laws

  • A Scout/Guide is a trustworthy
  • A Scout/Guide is a loyal
  • A Scout/Guide is a friend to all and a brother/sister to every other Scout/Guide.
  • A Scout/Guide is courteous
  • A Scout/Guide is a friend to animals and loves nature.
  • A Scout/Guide is disciplined and helps to protect public property.
  • A Scout/Guide is courageous.
  • A Scout/Guide is thrifty.
  • A Scout/Guide is pure in thought, word, and deed.
